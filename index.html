Designing Control System for Confocal Microscopy

June 2021 - May 2022

Built a customized Confocal Laser Scanning Microscopy (CLSM) system with an existing basic optical microscope in the laboratory. Starting with an outdated Olympus laser scan unit, we reverse-engineered the components and circuits inside and designed the new PCB board and the control system, using Altium Designer and LabVIEW as core development tools. The complete CLSM system consists of NI DAQ device, designed PCB for signal mapping and amplification, laser, mirror galvanometers, confocal pinhole structure, lenses, ND filters, BPFs, high voltage power source, PMT, and power transformers. Also in development of an automatic focus system for this CLSM. Motorized knob that controls the stage height will be placed and automatically adjusted in response to the acquired image resolution using Micromanager software and LabVIEW.

Single particle observations of CRISPR nanoplasmonic carriers

April 2021 - April 2022

Zhijia Zhang, Haechan Jeong, Di Zu, Xintao Zhao, Guangjie Cui, Single particle observations of CRISPR nanoplasmonic carriers, 2022 SPIE Photonics West The method of delivery is considered to be one of the significant hurdles for CRISPR technologies. Conventional delivery of CRISPR by viral vectors are subject to their immunogenicity. Non-viral vectors methods are preferable but are limited by their biocompatibility and precision. Nano-plasmonic particles have shown great potential as noninvasive and spatiotemporally controllable vehicles for biomacromolecule delivery. Hereby, as a safe and precise way to delivery of CRISPR, we propose a remote optically manipulating method based on gold nanorods carrier. We have characterized gold nano-plasmonic carriers on single particle level by imaging spectroscopy. Then we have demonstrated the precise manipulation of individual nano plasmonic carriers on cells using optical trapping system. Lastly, the biocompatibility of the method is shown.
